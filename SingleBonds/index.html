Single Bonds

Simple molecules form when two atoms share a pair of electrons. They overlap valence shells at just the right distance to keep the pair of electrons shared between both nuclei. If the nuclei are too close, their positive charges will repel, if the nuclei are too far apart the attraction between the positive nuclei and negative electrons isnâ€™t strong enough to form the bond. These molecules form single bonds where one electron from each atom is shared forming the single bond. This achieves a full valence shell for each atom.
