Ionic Bonding

Ions are formed when atoms gain or lose electrons to achieve full or stable valence shells of electrons. Metal atoms lose their valence electrons forming positive cations and the electrons are transferred to non-metal atoms that form negative anions.

The resulting lattice of cations and anions has no overall charge so the ratio of cations to anions will vary to achieve this depending on the charges of the ions involved. Multiple electrons can be transferred between atoms but the octet rule of filling valence shells on the non-metal atoms must be observed. Multiple non-metal atoms may be needed to accept all the donated electrons or multiple metal atoms may be needed to supply electrons to complete the valence shell. Explore the experiences to see the movement of electrons to form the charged ions.

The properties of ionic substances differ greatly from those of the raw elements.

Summary:

  • Takes place between metal and non-metal atoms.
  • Electrons are given and received by the atoms creating charged particles.
  • Giant lattices of these electrostatic ions are formed.
  • Positive ions are formed when atoms lose electrons – cations.
  • Negative ions are formed when atoms gain electrons – anions.
